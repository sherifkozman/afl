Research Concept

The Missing "Failure Mode"
in AI Agents

Current AI agents are designed to fulfill tasks regardless of capability, often leading to hallucinations. What if agents were harnessed to "give up" proactively? This research explores a controlled failure state as a mechanism for reliability.

Current State: "The Can-Do Trap"

Agents rarely admit inability. Without a harness, they attempt to bridge knowledge gaps with plausible-sounding fabrications (hallucinations).

Hypothesis: "Harnessed Failure"

Implementing a specific "Failure Mode" where the agent detects low confidence and proactively requests feedback instead of guessing.
